Climate Information Services · design diagnostic

Read your climate service as a designed service, dimension by dimension.

A guided interview for teams scoping or reviewing a Climate Information Service. It walks you through six design dimensions, records a self-assessment from 0 to 3 on each question, and returns a heuristic reading: where the service is under-designed, and the design brief each gap generates.

See how it works

About 15–20 minutes · English · no account needed · your answers stay in your browser.

The research behind it

The instrument comes from a study that read two operational Climate Information Services in Northern Ghana as designed services, then built a six-dimension diagnostic grid whose unanswered questions become design briefs. The distance between a forecast being available and a forecast being used is treated as a design distance, not an information one.

A formative instrument

The grid is used before deployment. A scoping team works through the questions, and every question it cannot answer marks a design decision still to be made.

An attention map, not a ranking

The radar shows which dimensions are under-designed within one service, so scarce attention goes where it is most needed. It does not rank services against each other.

Earned in the field

The questions were tested in four weeks of fieldwork in two farming communities, where a forecast reaches most farmers, and almost all women, through a human relay rather than a device.

Under revision by use

If you choose to contribute, your anonymous ratings keep the grid under revision and show which design knowledge teams most often lack. No personal data is ever collected.

How it works

Three stages, one sitting. You can speak your answers or type them, and you can leave and resume later.

1

Guided interview

Six dimensions. Each opens with a short explanation, then five questions with a field example. You rate each from 0 to 3.

2

Heuristic reading

Your scores are mapped onto a hexagonal radar and turned into a reading of strengths, weaknesses and the brief that closes each gap.

3

Downloadable report

Take the reading with you as a PDF. Your written notes never leave your device.

The six dimensions

A synthesis of vocabularies from service design, behavioural science and co-production research, combined into a single reading protocol for Climate Information Services.

Privacy policy

The instrument is built to need no personal data. This policy states exactly what happens, in line with the EU General Data Protection Regulation (Regulation 2016/679).

What the tool does with your answers

The interview runs entirely in your browser. Your ratings and your written notes are held on your device so that you can leave and resume, and they are cleared when you choose to start over. Your written notes are never transmitted anywhere, under any circumstance.

What is collected, and only with your consent

Before the interview begins you are asked whether you wish to contribute to the research. If you agree, and only then, the following is sent when you finish or leave: the thirty ratings from 0 to 3, the dimension you reached, whether you completed the interview, whether you downloaded the report, and a random session identifier generated in your browser. No name, no email address, no IP address and no free text are transmitted. The record cannot be traced back to you or to your organisation. If you decline, nothing is sent and the tool works exactly the same.

The purpose is the one stated in the underlying study: to keep the diagnostic grid under revision and to identify which design knowledge teams most often lack. The legal basis is your consent (Article 6(1)(a) GDPR), which you may withhold at any time by declining. Because the records carry no identifier linking them to a person, they cannot be individually retrieved or deleted after submission; this is an intentional consequence of collecting no personal data. Contributions are stored in a spreadsheet held by the data controller and are retained for the duration of the research.

Usage statistics

Visits are counted with GoatCounter, which sets no cookies, stores no personal data and does not track you across sites. This is why you see no cookie banner: the site sets no cookies for tracking.

Voice input

Answering by speaking is optional. If you use it on Chrome, Edge or Safari, your browser sends the audio to its own speech service (Google or Apple respectively) to transcribe it, in the same way as dictation elsewhere on your device, and their terms apply to that step. The site itself neither receives nor stores audio. You can switch voice off and type instead at any time. Having questions read aloud sends nothing anywhere.

Contact form

If you write to us through the form, the message and any email address you choose to give are transmitted by Web3Forms, which delivers them to the controller's mailbox. Use the email address above if you would rather not use the form.

Your rights

Where personal data exist, you have the right of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability and objection, and the right to lodge a complaint with the Italian Data Protection Authority (Garante per la protezione dei dati personali). In practice, the only personal data this site can hold is what you voluntarily write in the contact form.
